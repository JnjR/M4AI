Opportunity

If you are among the few who simply seek to know what AI is really all about, then this course is for you.

During my postdoctorate fellowship in explainability and trust in AI systems (2020 - 2023), I needed to teach myself the foundations of AI. After months building toy models with online code alongs, I had to accept that my understanding remained superficial. I was wasting time chasing short cuts. Then, out of desperation, I paid attention to the mathematical foundations of AI. In high school math we learn that the equation of a line can be written in slope-intercept form as \(y = mx + b\). An AI model is of the same form, except that the function is not known but discovered by the algorithm. Here, a function takes a dataset \(X \) as input into a function \(g \) to produce output \(y \); again, the power of an AI domain like machine learning is to deploy algorithms that can discover the function \(g \).

Motivation

The goal, always, is to be a critical thinker about the topic of interest in order to be an effective actor in the world. Citical Thinking is about logic and evidence, an the logic of AI for public health is mathematics. In this workshop, participants can expect to understand a model at the level of the function.

From personal experience, "machine learning is an obvious and direct motivation to learn mathematics" (Deisenroth et al., xii). What is more, a focus on the resolution of important problems like XXX does much to support the dual goals of accessibility and understanding in math education. Develop an addiction to wrestling with difficult ideas on the way to solving important problems like how to improve population level health outcomes. For those who may have not had the pleasure, this workshop provides, "Its ideas and problems are difficult and enticing" (Toeplitz, vii)

As a Black-led workshop open to all that was created as a social justice intervention, we must acknowledge the nasty undercurrents of AI that are roots in violence, oppresion and control. We hope that participants will practice being critical users and developers of AI. This why I launched the International Workshop on AI for De-escalation. The theme this year is Enhancing Human Security, Equality & Dignity to contniue the conversation about de-escalation as a feature rather than an after thought in automated systems.

Finally, we attempt to highlight in a few different places thoughout the potential harms, limitations, and social consequences of machine learning. From its roots in World War II, machine learning has always been political. Advances in artificial intelligence feed into a global industrial military complex, ans are funded by it. As useful as machine learning is for some unequivocally positive applications such as assistive devices, it is also used to great effect for tracking, surveillance, and warfare. Commercially its more successful use cases to date are targetted advertising and digital content recommendation, both of questionable value to society.
